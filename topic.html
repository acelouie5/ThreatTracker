What is
Virus?

Viruses are self-replicating programs that infect other files or systems by attaching themselves to them. They can cause damage to data, software, and hardware components.

Read More

What is
Worm?

Worms are similar to viruses but can spread independently without user intervention. They exploit vulnerabilities in computer networks and often consume network bandwidth or overload servers.

Read More

What is
Trojan Horse?

Trojans disguise themselves as legitimate software or files to trick users into executing them. Once installed, they can create backdoors, steal sensitive information, or enable remote access to the compromised system.

Read More

What is
Ransomware?

Ransomware encrypts the victim's files, making them inaccessible until a ransom is paid. It often spreads through malicious email attachments, compromised websites, or drive-by downloads.

Read More

What is
Spyware?

Spyware covertly collects information about a user's activities without their knowledge or consent. It can monitor keystrokes, capture screenshots, record browsing habits, and transmit the gathered data to a remote attacker.

Read More

What is
Adware?

Adware displays unwanted advertisements on the user's device, typically in the form of pop-ups or banners. It is often bundled with free software and can collect user information to deliver targeted ads.

Read More

What is
Botnets?

Botnets consist of a network of compromised computers, often called "zombies" or "bots." These infected machines are controlled remotely by a command and control server. Botnets are commonly used for large-scale attacks, such as distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks.

Read More

What is
Rootkits?

Rootkits are designed to hide malicious software or activities on a system by modifying the operating system or system utilities. They grant attackers unauthorized access and control over the compromised system.

Read More

What is
Keyloggers?

Keyloggers record keystrokes on a computer or mobile device. They can capture sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, credit card details, and other personal data.

Read More

What is
Fileless Malware?

Fileless malware operates directly in computer memory without leaving traces on the hard drive. It leverages legitimate system processes or vulnerabilities to carry out its malicious activities, making it difficult to detect.
